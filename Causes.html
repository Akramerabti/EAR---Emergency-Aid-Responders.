Mission Statement

Emergency Aid Responders (E.A.R.) is a nongovernmental organization committed to providing immediate and long-term relief to the affected communities in the aftermath of natural disasters. Our goal is to provide essential humanitarian aid, including food, water, medical care, and other critical services that are necessary for survival and recovery. We believe in the principles of dignity and respect. We strive to collaborate with local organizations, communities, and governments to build sustainable solutions and promote self-reliance among the people we serve. Our agency is guided by a strong sense of responsibility to the affected communities, and we are dedicated to maximizing the impact of our work by having a strategic plan and effective management. EAR is committed to transparency, accountability and is open to collaborations with other agencies in the filed



Why was Haiti vulnerable to begin with?

After Haiti`s independence from France, its development has been very slow due to a colonial era debt it had to pay to the colonizers. This debt has been estimated to be approximately equal to 80% of Haiti`s revenue (the debt is estimated to be equivalent of $21 billion today). The foreign powers intervention has resulted Haiti to be underdeveloped in many sections such as infrastructure. The origins of the crises are rooted in historical events and are driven by economic and political instability as well as meteorological hazards` impacts.


Info on Haitian earthquake (including criteria, maps, stats)



The initial shock of the earthquake has been estimated to have a magnitude 7.0 and it was followed by aftershocks of magnitudes between 5.9 and 5.5. It is estimated that three million people are affected by the earthquake with the approximate death toll at 200000 (the death toll is difficult ton determine due to the shortage of heavy lifting equipment). This earthquake left approximately one million homeless. Due to the destruction of many hospitals, the survivors have to wait days to get assistance. Because of the destruction of parts of the international airports in Port-au-Prince, sending assistance and supplies with airplanes is slower than usual.
